I have heard him on such occasions produce sounds of a nature similar to what may be sometimes heard in Dutch clocks.  Not,’ said Mrs. Sparsit, with a lofty sense of giving strict evidence, ‘that I would convey any imputation on his moral character.  Far from it.  I have always considered Bitzer a young man of the most upright principle; and to that I beg to bear my testimony.’
