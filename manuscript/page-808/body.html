I ha’ read on ’t in the public petition, as onny one may read, fro’ the men that works in pits, in which they ha’ pray’n and pray’n the lawmakers for Christ’s sake not to let their work be murder to ’em, but to spare ’em for th’ wives and children that they loves as well as gentlefok loves theirs.  When it were in work, it killed wi’out need; when ’tis let alone, it kills wi’out need.  See how we die an’ no need, one way an’ another—in a muddle—every day!’

He faintly said it, without any anger against any one.  Merely as the truth.
