Apparently so long an interval ensued with the men at the windlass standing idle, that some women shrieked that another accident had happened!  But the surgeon who held the watch, declared five minutes not to have elapsed yet, and sternly admonished them to keep silence.  He had not well done speaking, when the windlass was reversed and worked again.  Practised eyes knew that it did not go as heavily as it would if both workmen had been coming up, and that only one was returning.

The rope came in tight and strained; and ring after ring was coiled upon the barrel of the windlass, and all eyes were fastened on the pit.  The sobered man was brought up and leaped out briskly on the grass.  There was an universal cry of ‘Alive or dead?’ and then a deep, profound hush.
